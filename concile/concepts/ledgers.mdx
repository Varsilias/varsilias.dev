title: "Ledgers"
description: "Understand how Concile treats ledgers and operational systems during reconciliation."
---

Concile does not replace your ledger. It compares ledger expectations against records from processors, banks, queues, or internal systems.

## Ledger role

The ledger is usually the expected side of reconciliation. It should describe what the business believes happened.

## Operational role

Operational systems describe what was observed: processor transactions, settlement files, webhook events, or database rows.

## Common drift

- The processor has a transaction the ledger never recorded.
- The ledger has an entry for a transaction that failed upstream.
- Amounts differ because of fees, rounding, or currency conversion.
- Events arrive late and appear in different settlement windows.
