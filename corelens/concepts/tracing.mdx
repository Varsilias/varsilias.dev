---
title: "Tracing"
description: "Use traces to follow request and job execution through Corelens instrumentation."
---

Tracing explains what happened during one unit of work. In Corelens, a trace is made of spans that describe the request, job, or operation path.

## Span shape

A useful span has a name, timing, status, and attributes.

```javascript
await corelens.span("invoice.reconcile", async span => {
  span.setAttribute("invoice.id", invoice.id);
  span.setAttribute("invoice.currency", invoice.currency);
  await reconcileInvoice(invoice);
});
```

## Naming spans

Use names that describe the operation, not the implementation detail.

| Prefer | Avoid |
|--------|-------|
| `invoice.reconcile` | `runFunction` |
| `payment.authorize` | `stripeCall` |
| `report.generate` | `loopRows` |

## Attributes

Attributes make traces searchable. Keep values low-cardinality unless you have a specific debugging need.

<Warning>
  Do not put secrets, access tokens, session IDs, or full payloads in span attributes.
</Warning>
