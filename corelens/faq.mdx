## Is Corelens a replacement for OpenTelemetry?

No. Corelens is an application-level instrumentation layer that should work with OpenTelemetry-compatible export paths. Use OpenTelemetry directly if your team already has a complete setup and does not need a smaller wrapper.

## Does Corelens store telemetry?

No. Corelens emits telemetry from your service. Storage, querying, and retention belong to your collector or observability backend.

## Can I use Corelens without a vendor?

Yes. Use the console exporter locally or send OTLP telemetry to your own OpenTelemetry collector.

## Should every function become a span?

No. Instrument operations that explain system behavior: external calls, queue work, reconciliation steps, expensive computations, and meaningful domain transitions.

## Is the SDK stable?

Yes. Treat the documented SDK contract as stable for application integration. Breaking changes should include migration notes before release.
